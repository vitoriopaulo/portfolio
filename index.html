About



I am a Brazilian-American Architect & Designer. I've experienced a wide range of design genres in my trajectory, such as: Architecture, Furniture, Interiors, Communication, and Webdesign as well. Nowadays I devote efforts to improve skills in web-development, both front and back-end development, as well as the matters related to UX|UI design, Information Architecture and usability. I am based in Boston, MA. USA since 2010, and just turned a General Assembly Boston alumni from the WDI (Web Development Immersive) class 020.

It was a life changing experience: I've developed tech skills fast and learned state-of-the-art technologies of the industry: HTML5, CSS, SCSS, JAVASCRIPT, jQuery, Ajax, Ruby, Ruby on rails, SQL, PostegreSQL, GIT, GITHUB, HEROKU and more mentored by a talented team of web dev practictioners. At this point of my life I've already accomplished my goals as an Architect and Designer and I am looking straight ahead for a career shift into the tech industry.

Based in Boston but with my eyes in the world, my goal as a Full-stack Web-developer is to design products that are functional, aesthetically pleasant and that establish a strong emotional connection with the web user. That tells all about my vision, my mission, my passion, my dreams.

FileDumpling, the third project of the WDI 020 Fall Class Boston is a full-stack SPA (Single Page web Application) where registered users can upload and download files. The uploaded files are hosted with Amazon Web Services' S3 Bucket, and the downloaded files are backed-up in the user's local computer. For each file added, metadata is stored, using a back-end application. Users can edit information about the files they upload themselves, but not for files uploaded by other users. The front-end of the app was built with Html5, Css3, Javascript, jQuery, Ajax, Handlebars, Bootstrap. The back-end was developed on top of Node.Js, Express.Js, MongoDb, Moogoose, AWS S3.
